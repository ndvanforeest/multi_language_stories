<fr>Pendant ce temps-là, l'armée des bourdons et celle des abeilles combattaient l'armée des oiseaux. Tous avaient repris leurs formes magiques, et les hommes virent avec horreur une bataille dont ils n'avaient jamais eu l'idée. Des insectes aussi grands que des hommes luttaient avec rage contre des oiseaux dont le moindre était aussi gros qu'un éléphant. Les terribles dards des bêtes piquantes atteignaient parfois les flancs sensibles des alouettes, des fauvettes et des colombes; mais les mésanges adroites dévoraient les abeilles par milliers, les aigles en abattaient cent d'un coup d'aile, les casoars présentaient leurs casques impénétrables à leurs traits empoisonnés, et l''oiseau armé', qui a un grand éperon acéré à chaque épaule, embrochait vingt ennemis à la minute.
<en>Meanwhile, the army of drones and bees fought the army of birds. All had resumed their magical forms, and the men saw with horror a battle of which they had never had the idea. Insects as tall as men fought rage against birds, the smallest of which was as big as an elephant. The terrible darts of the prickly beasts sometimes reached the sensitive sides of larks, warblers and doves; but the clever tits devoured the bees by the thousands, the eagles slaughtered a hundred of them with a blow of the wing, the cassowaries presented their helmets impenetrable to their poisoned features, and the armed bird, which has a large sharp spur at each shoulder, Twenty enemies by the minute.

<en>After having contemplated this hideous sight, the crowd turned towards the pyre of Gribouille, which offered nothing but a mountain of ashes; but at the summit of this mountain there was a beautiful flower, which is called 'souvenez-de-moi'. <en>Since that day, the inhabitants of the country of Gribouille lived very happy under the protection of the queen of the meadows, and a temple was raised in memory of Gribouille. On that day he was ordered by the laws of the kingdom to terminate all disputes and to forgive all wrongs and insults.
